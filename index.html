Established in 1982, the Institute for Cultural Research and Action (ICRA) is dedicated to advancing sustainable agricultural practices for small-scale farmers in rain-fed regions. Our primary focus lies in fostering equitable methodologies, facilitating knowledge exchange, and strengthening community-led initiatives to enhance resilience and prosperity in rural communities through a robust environmental and ecological approach.

ICRA'S FOCUS
Growing Futures Together: ICRAs participatory initiatives aimed at fostering sustainable agriculture, ecological security, and rural empowerment.
The realisation of sustainable agricultural practices hinges on several pivotal components. These entail providing training in sustainable agriculture, the timely dissemination of site-specific information, and the accessibility of essential inputs. ICRA organises practical training programs emphasising organic farming, permaculture, agroforestry, water and soil conservation, and mixed cropping. These concerted endeavours enhance farmers' inclination and assurance in embracing sustainable agricultural methodologies. (( combines sustainable Ag+ Farmers exper ,Out reach+community development)
In regions where small-scale farmers rely on rain-fed agriculture, there is a strong emphasis on promoting agricultural practices that prioritise biodiversity and sustainability. This approach involves the integration of ecological principles with economic viability. It encompasses implementing strategies to adapt to and mitigate the impacts of climate change within the agricultural sector. Key strategies include the promotion of agroforestry, which involves intentionally integrating trees and shrubs into crop and animal farming systems to create environmental, economic, and social benefits. Additionally, efforts are made to cultivate drought-resistant crops that can thrive in water-stressed environments, adopt soil conservation techniques to prevent erosion and maintain soil fertility, and utilise water harvesting methods to capture and store rainfall for agricultural use. ( combines biodiverse cropping+ climate change)

Our organisation is deeply committed to the cause of sustainable agriculture, as evidenced by our comprehensive support to 12,000 small farm families spanning 15,000 acres. We focus on biodiverse organic farming and reintroducing mixed cropping utilising local crop varieties. Notably, in Kotturu, our initiatives have successfully encouraged farmers to embrace organic multi-crop systems, boasting an impressive 90% participation rate from farm families. ( combines comprehensive support and proven impact)
Farmers' insights and experiences predominantly drive sustainable agriculture. A critical undertaking is to underscore and facilitate the dissemination of these experiences, fostering farmer-led innovations across various agricultural practices, encompassing farm design, crop management, and beyond. The active involvement of farming communities in advocating for policy amendments that favour small-scale farmers, alongside their participation in networks and partnerships to amplify the voice of rural communities in decision-making processes, is an enduring endeavour. ( combines innovation and policy)

What makes ICRA distinct?

Through biodiverse sustainable organic farming practices, farmers can achieve comparable or superior yields in twelve key crops under rainfed conditions, including select horticulture crops like tomatoes, green chillies, brinjal, okra, and banana. At ICRA, we recognize farmers as experts, valuing their indigenous knowledge and lived experience as the foundation of organic biodiverse farming. Our approach centres on collaborating with farmers through the Prayog Parivar experimental group, a dynamic platform for shared learning, innovation, and experimentation. ICRA embraces an inclusive approach, welcoming diverse alternative practices such as organic farming, agroecology, permaculture, quarter-acre farming, LEISA, biodynamic farming, and natural farming. We are committed to learning from and adapting to local farmers' unique needs, fostering a culture of openness, innovation, and context-specific solutions.
