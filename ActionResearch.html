Action-Research

At ICRA, we believe that rainfed farming sustains millions, yet faces numerous challenges. Our action-research initiative focuses on understanding and transforming these complex dynamics to create positive changes in agriculture. Land, the primary production site, is vulnerable to numerous factors such as market fluctuations, labor issues, and environmental stressors that affect small farmers' livelihoods and the environment. By conducting research, we aim to address these challenges with evidence-based solutions, ensuring a sustainable food system for all.

Unlike conventional academic studies, our approach is participatory, dynamic, and solution-focused. Through continuous field investigations, we have tackled issues like food security, seed sovereignty, small farm economics, and more. Our findings are shared openly in our in-house magazine to promote transparency and collective knowledge.

Key Research Areas

Research with Farmers

We conduct participatory research with farmers to identify and tackle specific challenges in organic farming under rainfed conditions. Our aim is to provide actionable insights that benefit both farmers and the environment.

Documenting Local Practices

By studying and documenting local knowledge and traditional practices related to organic farming, we preserve vital farming techniques that are well-suited to rainfed agriculture.

Innovative Solutions

Our focus is on developing and testing innovative solutions such as organic manures, pest management strategies, and the conservation of indigenous seeds to improve farming sustainability.

Policy and Practice Change

We analyze our research findings to advocate for informed policy and practice changes that support sustainable farming and better livelihoods for farmers across the region.
